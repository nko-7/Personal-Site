About Me

My journey to become a programmer has been a long road. Years ago when I first started my degree at the University of Waterloo I barely knew coding was a thing. I was enamoured with studying science I didn't really look elsewhere. Little did I know coding was a huge part of Physics and everything else. I took some computer science classes at Waterloo, but despite realizing a love of computers and coding decided to keep with my degree.

I ended up getting a job at a software company in a project management role of all things. There I realized that programming was a lucrative proffession and something I could see myself doing. I decided to try and take a path towards the goal of becoming a programmer, but unfortunately was let go from that company.

I questioned my future and if I should try and take advantage of my degree or switch streams and commit to being a programmer. I ended up enrolling at Seneca so I could have a more structured learning environment and learn as much as possible. Hopefully in the future I can land a role doing data analysis or data science so I can still take advantage of the skills I learned with my degree and all the programming experience I will gain at Seneca.
